# ⚡ Caching (Redis)

## Simple Explanation
Imagine you are at an office. You have a **big file cabinet** (The Database) in the other room and a **small desk** (The Cache) in front of you. 
If you need a paper 100 times a day, you don't go to the other room 100 times. You just keep that paper on your desk for fast access!

---

## What
Caching is the technique of storing a **copy** of your most popular data in a super-fast, temporary storage (like RAM) so you don't have to ask the slow database every time.

---

## Why
1.  **Speed (Low Latency):** Databases are slow because they read from a hard disk. Caches (like Redis) are fast because they read from RAM (memory).
2.  **To Protect the Database:** If 1 million people are looking at the same celebrity photo, we don't want 1 million requests hitting our database. We store it once in the cache and serve it from there.
3.  **To Save Money:** Fast databases are expensive. Caching allows you to serve more users with a smaller database.

---

## How it works
1.  **Request:** A user asks for a profile: "Get User #10".
2.  **Check Cache (Hit/Miss):** 
    *   **Cache Hit:** If the data is in the cache, the user gets it instantly. (0.01 seconds!)
    *   **Cache Miss:** If it’s not there, the server goes to the Database (Slower, 1 second).
3.  **Populate Cache:** After getting data from the database, the server "saves a copy" in the Cache for the next person.

---

## Real-World Example
*   **Instagram Profile:** When you click on your own profile, Instagram stores it in a cache. If you hit "Back" and then "My Profile" again, it loads in 0.001 seconds from the Cache!
*   **Weather Apps:** The weather in London doesn't change every second. Instead of checking a weather station 1,000 times, we check it once every 10 minutes and "Cache" the result for everyone to see.

---

## Key Terms
*   **Redis:** The most popular "Cache" software in the world.
*   **TTL (Time To Live):** How long the data stays on your "desk" before you throw it away to get a new copy.
*   **Eviction Policy:** When your "desk" is full, which paper do you throw away? (Usually the oldest/least-used one).

---

## Interview Questions
**1. What is a "Cache Hit" vs "Cache Miss"?**
*   **A:** A "Hit" means the data was found in the cache. A "Miss" means the data was not there and we had to go to the main database.

**2. Why is Redis so fast?**
*   **A:** Because it stores data in **RAM** (memory) instead of a hard disk. RAM is much faster but more expensive.

**3. What is "Cache Invalidation"?**
*   **A:** It’s the process of deleting or updating the data in the cache when the original source (the database) changes.

**4. What is the "Least Recently Used" (LRU) policy?**
*   **A:** When the cache is full, it deletes the data that hasn't been accessed for the longest time to make room for new data.

**5. Can we use a Cache as our main Database?**
*   **A:** Usually no, because RAM is "volatile" (it loses data if the power goes out). We only use it as a temporary copy of the main database.
