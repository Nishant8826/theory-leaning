# 🧱 System Design Basics

## Simple Explanation
Imagine you are running a small lemonade stand. On the first day, only 2 people come. It’s easy! But what if 1,000 people come tomorrow? You need to think about how to handle them, how to make sure you never run out of lemons, and what happens if your juicer breaks. **System Design** is exactly that—but for apps and websites!

---

## 🔝 Scalability
### What
Scalability is the ability of your system to handle more and more users without crashing or slowing down.

### Why
If your app becomes famous (like Instagram), you will go from 10 users to 10 million. If your system isn't scalable, it will "hang" or crash, and users will leave.

### How
There are two ways to scale:
1.  **Vertical Scaling:** Buying a bigger juice machine (more RAM/CPU in one computer).
2.  **Horizontal Scaling:** Buying 5 more small juice machines (adding more computers to share the work).

---

## 🛡️ Reliability
### What
Reliability means the system works correctly even if something goes wrong (like a server breaking or a bug appearing).

### Why
If a user "Likes" a post, it must be saved. If the database crashes, we shouldn't lose that "Like." A reliable system never loses your data.

### How
We achieve this through **Redundancy** (keeping copies). If one server fails, another copy of the data is ready to take over.

---

## ⏱️ Availability
### What
Availability is the percentage of time your app is "Up" and running. You might have heard of "99.9% uptime."

### Why
If WhatsApp is down for even 5 minutes, the whole world notices! High availability ensures your app is always ready when the user opens it.

### How
We use multiple servers in different locations. If one city has a power cut, the servers in another city keep the app alive.

---

## 🤝 Consistency
### What
Consistency means every user should see the **same data** at the same time.

### Why
If you change your Instagram profile picture, you don't want your friend to see the old one while you see the new one. Everyone should be on the same page.

### How
Data is synchronized across all servers so they all show the latest version.

---

## 🌍 Real-World Example: Amazon Sale
*   **Scalability:** During "Prime Day," millions of people visit at once. Amazon adds thousands of temporary servers to handle the crowd.
*   **Availability:** Even if one warehouse's server fails, you can still buy items because other servers are running.
*   **Reliability:** Once you pay, your order is never lost, even if there is a network glitch.

## Interview Questions
**1. What is Scalability?**
*   **A:** It is the system's ability to handle increasing amounts of work (more users/data) by adding resources.

**2. Difference between Availability and Reliability?**
*   **A:** Availability is about the system being "Up" (reachable). Reliability is about the system performing "Correctly" (not losing data).

**3. What is Redundancy?**
*   **A:** It means keeping extra copies of data or servers so that if one fails, the "redundant" one can take over.

**4. Why is Vertical Scaling limited?**
*   **A:** Because you can only make one computer so big. Eventually, you run out of slots for more RAM or CPUcores.

**5. What is 99.9% Uptime?**
*   **A:** It means the system is only allowed to be down for about 8.7 hours in an entire year!
