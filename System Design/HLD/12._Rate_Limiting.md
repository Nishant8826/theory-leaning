# 🛑 Rate Limiting

## Simple Explanation
Imagine a very popular water fountain. If one person tries to fill a giant 1,000-liter tank, no one else can drink! 
To fix this, you put a rule: **"Only 1 liter per person per minute."** 
**Rate Limiting** is exactly that—but for your app. It protects your app from a "Hacker" or a "Bot" that is trying to click the button 1,000 times in 1 second.

---

## What
Rate Limiting is a technique to limit the number of requests a user (or a bot) can make to your server in a specific amount of time.

---

## Why
1.  **Security:** It stops hackers from trying to "Guess" a password 1 million times.
2.  **To Save Resources:** One "Bot" can take up all your RAM and CPU, making the app slow for real humans.
3.  **Cost:** If you pay for ogni "Request," you don't want a "Loop" in your code to spend $1,000 by mistake!

---

## How it works (The Algorithms)
1.  **Fixed Window:** You have a "Bucket" that only allows 100 requests every 1 minute. At the 61st second, the bucket clears and you get 100 more. (But what if the user sends 100 on the 59th second and 100 on the 61st second? That's too fast!)
2.  **Sliding Window:** Imagine a "Moving Bucket" that only looks at the **last 60 seconds** from this exact moment. (More accurate).
3.  **Token Bucket (The Pizza Dealer):** You give out "Tokens" at a slow speed (e.g., 1 per second). A user must have a "Token" to make a request. If they use them all, they have to wait for the next token to arrive.

---

## Real-World Example
*   **Instagram Login:** Try putting a wrong password 10 times in a row. Instagram will say: *"Too many attempts. Please try again in 30 minutes."* This is Rate Limiting protecting your account.
*   **API Sales:** If you use the "Google Maps API," they allow you to search 10,000 times for free, but then they "Rate Limit" you and ask for money.

## Interview Questions
**1. What is Rate Limiting?**
*   **A:** It is the process of controlling the amount of traffic sent or received by a network or server.

**2. Which algorithm is best for "Smooth" traffic?**
*   **A:** **Token Bucket** or **Leaky Bucket** are best because they allow for small sudden bursts while keeping the long-term speed steady.

**3. Where should you put the Rate Limiter?**
*   **A:** In the **API Gateway** or as a separate **Middleware** before the request hits the actual Database or Service.

**4. What is the standard HTTP response code for Rate Limiting?**
*   **A:** **429 Too Many Requests**.

**5. How to identify a user to rate limit them?**
*   **A:** Usually by their **IP Address**, their **User ID**, or their **API Key**.
